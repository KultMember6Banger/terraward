# Giving Back — trees, the Grove, and honest thanks

> **Status: the sustainable half of the rewards idea, captured.** The prize-contest half (year-end
> vacations / cash / gadgets) is deliberately **deferred** and flagged at the end — it's a
> regulated promotion that needs legal review and a separate budget, and it must never be tied to
> tips. Everything in the main body here is safe, sustainable, and on-brand.

The spirit: **give back, plant something living, and honour the people who help** — done inside
TerraWard's covenant (no surveillance, no gambling-law traps, no pressure to monetise).

---

## 1 € = 1 tree

Every tip, even the smallest — **at least 1 €** — plants a tree. Supporting the tool becomes
something good for the actual planet, which is about as on-theme as it gets.

Two things keep this trustworthy rather than a hollow promise:

- **Verified planting.** Partner with an established reforestation organisation that issues
  receipts or a public registry, *or* run a public counter backed by proof — so the trees are
  real and checkable, not just claimed.
- **Decoupled from everything else.** Tipping plants a tree and earns recognition. It **never**
  buys standing, points, or eligibility for any prize. (That separation isn't just principle —
  it's what keeps the whole thing on the right side of the law; see the deferred section.)

---

## The Grove — the supporters list

Anyone who tips earns a place in **the Grove**: a public roll of supporters, with trees planted
in their honour. *(Working name — yours to change.)*

- Being named is **opt-in**; anonymous tipping is always welcome and counts the same.
- Recognition — your name, your trees, a thank-you — not a jackpot. This is the healthy kind of
  reward: it costs the project almost nothing and it can't be gamed or corrupted.

---

## Transparency — trust, applied to money

The code is open; the money should be too.

- A **public ledger**: tips in, and exactly where they go (trees planted, and any running costs
  like a relay server).
- A simple, checkable statement — *"4,000 tips → 4,000 trees, planted here"* — verifiable through
  the planting partner or registry.

A promise no one can check erodes the very trust the gesture is meant to build. Make it checkable.

---

## The yearly thank-you

Once a year, a moment of recognition:

- **Supporters** (the Grove) and the **top *corroborated* contributors** — the people whose shared
  signals proved accurate, drawn from the Commons reputation, which is anonymised and verifiable
  rather than surveilled.
- A small, on-theme token — a packet of seeds, a sticker, perhaps the icon as an enamel pin one
  day. Almost no cost, genuinely personal.

Recognition and a story, not a prize pool. Sustainable forever.

---

## What we deliberately do *not* do (and why)

**Points for "how much you use the app" or "how much you tend your own farm."**
This sounds great but it fights TerraWard's core promise. To score your usage or your private
farm activity, the system would have to either **watch you** (phone-home telemetry — the
surveillance this project exists to avoid) or **trust your self-report** (trivially gamed by
looping the app or faking inputs). You can't have "the system sees your activity" and "the system
sees nothing" at once. So we reward only **what's visible without spying**:

- **tips** — there's a payment record, no surveillance needed; and
- **corroborated Commons contributions** — the reputation already designed, anonymised and
  checkable.

Your private usage and own-farm work stay exactly that: private.

## Deferred — the big year-end prizes (needs legal review, later)

A contest where points accumulate and the top 10–20 win **vacations, gadgets, or cash** is a
**regulated promotion**, and in the EU/Belgium that's serious. Captured here so it isn't lost,
and fenced so it isn't built carelessly:

- **Gambling-law landmine.** If **tipping money raises standing toward a cash or travel prize**,
  that resembles the classic illegal-lottery test — *payment + chance + prize*. This is why real
  promotions carry a "no purchase necessary" free-entry route.
- **Tax.** Travel and cash prizes are usually **taxable for the winner**.
- **Cross-border.** An EU-wide draw multiplies the rules country by country, and may need
  published terms and a licence.

**If it's ever done**, it must be a properly-structured **skill** contest (won on deterministic
merit, not chance), with **free entry**, **published terms**, **tax handled**, **a lawyer's
review**, and **completely decoupled from tips** — never "tip more to rank higher." Not a
near-term feature. *(This is not legal advice; it's the reason to get some.)*

**And the economics:** a week or two of vacation for 10–20 people, plus gadgets and cash, is
easily many thousands of euros a year. Small tree-tips fund trees and maybe a server — not a prize
pool that size. Chasing that budget is exactly the pressure that pushes free projects toward ads
or data deals: the capture TerraWard is built to avoid. Recognition-and-trees lasts forever; the
jackpot model needs its own real funding and creates a pull you don't want.
